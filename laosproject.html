Legacy of War in Laos

A bomb was dropped every 8 minutes, every 24 hours, for 9 years.

The Bombs

From 1964 to 1973, the U.S. dropped more ordanance on Laos than all the ordanance dropped on all countries during WWII.

Over 270 million cluster bombs were dropped on Laos during the Vietnam War (210 million more bombs than were dropped on Iraq in 1991, 1998 and 2006 combined); up to 80 million did not detonate.

At least 20,000 people have been killed or injured by unexploded ordnance in Laos since the Vietnam War-era bombings ended.

The UXO problem

Laos is one of the poorest countries in the world today. The people are unable to farm the land for fear of triggering an UXO.

Many children seek UXO for the value of scrap metal could mean the difference between eating and not eating.

Making a Difference

UCT6, an all-female UXO clearance team, works non-stop in freeing up land for farming. As a result, more than a half million people gained land that is safe to walk, play, and work on

Over the past ten years alone, MAG (mine action organization) has made around 44 million metres of land safe from unexploded cluster munitions, and destroyed more than 180,000 deadly explosive devices.

Legacies of War, a Washington-based nonprofit, successfully advocates $12 million for the clearing of UXO in Laos.
